**Yes, in a skills-based tech economy, a decade of hands-on experience as a computer engineer running your own company is generally far more valuable to most employers than a job certificate alone.**

### Why Experience Wins (Especially Yours)
- **Real-world impact beats signals**: Employers in software/computer engineering prioritize what you can *do*—problem-solving, shipping products, handling ambiguity, working with stakeholders, debugging in production, and delivering business results. Ten years building and maintaining systems for your own company demonstrates ownership, end-to-end responsibility, and practical expertise that certificates rarely match.
- **Skills-based hiring trends**: A growing majority of employers (70%+ in recent surveys) use skills-based practices, focusing on competencies over degrees or credentials. Tech leaders like Google, IBM, Apple, and Microsoft have de-emphasized formal education in favor of demonstrable skills. However, this often means *proven* abilities via portfolios, projects, or track records—not just paper credentials.
- **Self-employment is a strength**: Running your own company shows initiative, versatility (wearing multiple hats), resilience, and entrepreneurial problem-solving. These translate well to many roles, especially smaller teams or companies valuing autonomy. Frame it on your resume with quantifiable achievements (e.g., "Built and scaled X feature serving Y users, reducing costs by Z%").

### Where a Certificate Can Still Help
Certificates (e.g., AWS, Google Career Certificates, CompTIA, Azure, specific frameworks) are useful for:
- Proving familiarity with current tools/technologies.
- Filling gaps if your experience is in older stacks.
- Getting past initial ATS screens or for specialized roles (cybersecurity, cloud, etc.).

But they are **supplements**, not substitutes, for substantial experience. A certificate without supporting work history is often seen as entry-level signaling.

### Practical Realities in 2026 Tech Hiring
- **Experience > Everything for mid/senior roles**: Hiring managers value proven delivery and impact. Your decade of self-directed work can outperform a recent certificate holder with limited practice.
- **Portfolio and storytelling matter**: GitHub repos, case studies, client results, or a personal site showcasing your projects will carry more weight than a cert. Be ready to discuss challenges, trade-offs, and outcomes in interviews.
- **Potential caveats**: Some large enterprises or regulated industries may still have formal requirements (degrees, specific certs, or security clearances). Self-employment experience sometimes needs stronger framing to prove collaboration/team skills. The market remains competitive, so tailor applications.
- **Combination is ideal**: Your experience + targeted modern certs (if needed for new tech) + strong portfolio = very competitive profile.

**Bottom line**: In a true skills-based economy, your track record of delivering as a computer engineer for a decade is a major advantage. Focus on translating that experience into clear, results-oriented narratives and evidence that employers can verify. Certificates can help round it out, but they won't overshadow strong, relevant work history.
